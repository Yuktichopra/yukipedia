About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the International School for Advanced Studies (SISSA) in Trieste, Italy. My research focuses on the neural and computational basis of decision-making. I am passionate about science communication and making science accessible to everyone.

Research

My research investigates the neural mechanisms of economic decision-making using EEG and computational modeling. I am particularly interested in how the brain represents and evaluates choices, and how these processes are influenced by cognitive biases.
